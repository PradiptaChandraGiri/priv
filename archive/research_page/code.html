Alex Chen (Me), Dr. Sarah Jenkins, Michael O'Connor

IEEE Transactions on Industrial Informatics

This paper proposes a novel federated learning architecture tailored for resource-constrained Internet of Things (IoT) devices. Optimizing Microservice Latency via Predictive Autoscaling using Transformer Models

Alex Chen (Me), David Kim

Submitted to ACM Symposium on Cloud Computing (SoCC)

Reactive autoscaling in cloud-native microservices often suffers from 'cold start' latency spikes during sudden traffic bursts. We introduce an intelligent scaling orchestrator utilizing a lightweight Transformer-based time-series forecasting model. Evaluated against a simulated Kubernetes cluster running a standard e-commerce benchmark, our predictive approach preemptively scales pods, reducing 99th percentile response times by up to 28% compared to standard CPU-threshold HPA configurations.

I. Introduction

The proliferation of Internet of Things (IoT) devices has led to an exponential increase in data generation at the network edge. Traditional centralized machine learning paradigms require transmitting raw data to cloud servers, raising significant privacy concerns and encountering bandwidth limitations.

Federated Learning (FL) emerges as a promising paradigm to address these challenges by training models locally on client devices and only exchanging model updates.

However, applying FL to IoT environments presents unique hurdles, primarily due to the severe resource constraints (compute, memory, battery) of edge devices and the high heterogeneity of data distribution.

In this work, we propose a specialized FL framework that incorporates a lightweight differential privacy mechanism paired with an adaptive compression scheme tailored for intermittent connectivity.
